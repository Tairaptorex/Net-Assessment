Visiting A Marae

A simple guide for students

Return to Teacher View?

Welcome!

A marae is a very special place for Māori.

It is a place where people:

  • Come together
  • Share stories
  • Learn
  • Celebrate
  • Remember their ancestors

When we visit, we show manaakitanga, which is kindness and respect.

Top tip: A marae is like a home, a school, and a community space all in one.

Before You Go

We get ready before visiting a marae.

  • Arrive on time
  • Wear tidy clothes
  • Listen to your teacher

Top tip: Turn off your phone and give your full attention.

Going Onto the Marae

We do not walk onto the marae by ourselves.

Important: Wait to be welcomed on.

  • Walk together as a group
  • Stay quiet
  • Listen carefully

You might see or hear:

  • Karanga (calling)
  • Speeches
  • Singing
  • Haka

Afterwards, we greet people by:

  • Shaking hands
  • Doing a hongi (pressing noses)

Top tip: Just follow your teacher and others around you.

Inside the Wharenui

The wharenui is a very special place.

Before going in:

  • Take off your shoes

Inside:

  • Walk calmly
  • Speak quietly
  • No food or drinks
  • Be respectful

A marae is very important, so we show our best behaviour.

Remember

  • Be respectful
  • Listen carefully
  • Follow instructions
  • Be kind to others

Important: Do not take photos unless you are told it is okay.


It might feel like a lot, but don’t worry.

If you try your best and show respect, you will have an amazing experience!
